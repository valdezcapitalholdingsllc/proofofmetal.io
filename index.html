Asset Explanations

What each token claims to represent

PAX Gold (PAXG)

Issued by Paxos, each token is backed by one fine troy ounce of a London Good Delivery gold bar stored in Brink’s vaults.

Tether Gold (XAUT)

Managed by Tether, each token represents one troy ounce of gold on a physical bar, with ownership tracked on the blockchain.

Kinesis Gold (KAU)

Part of the Kinesis platform, these tokens are fully allocated and redeemable for physical gold.

Comtech Gold (CGO)

Tokens are backed by physical gold with high liquidity.

Kinesis Silver (KAG)

Each token represents 1 ounce of silver, offering potential yields to holders.

Meld Gold (GOLD$)

Mints tokens on the Algorand blockchain, with each token representing 1 gram of gold, focusing on low transaction fees.

VNX Gold (VNXAU)

A Liechtenstein-based token backed by 1 gram of LBMA-certified gold.

Perth Mint Gold Token (PMGT)

Backed by the Government of Western Australia, this token is highly regulated and often features zero storage fees.

AurusGOLD (AWG)

A token supported by 1 gram of 99.99% LBMA-certified gold.

Kinka (XNK)

A Japanese token launched in 2024 on Ethereum, linked to 1 gram of gold.

Key Considerations

Before you trust a “metal-backed” token

Redeemability

Most reputable tokens allow for redemption, though minimum amounts may apply (e.g., 200 ounces for KAG).

Transparency

Look for projects that provide regular, independent third-party audits of their physical reserves.

Storage & Regulation

Gold is usually stored in secure, insured vaults (e.g., in Switzerland or London) and sometimes regulated by entities like the NYDFS.

Risks

Investors face risks related to vault security, the bankruptcy of the issuer, or fraudulent projects that do not hold adequate reserves.
