Asset Explanations

Plain-language summaries

The notes below summarize how each project describes its backing and redemption model. Always verify details directly with issuer documentation and independent audits.

PAX Gold (PAXG)

Paxos issues PAXG as a tokenized gold product. Each token is marketed as representing one fine troy ounce of London Good Delivery gold held in professional vault custody.

Tether Gold (XAUT)

Tether positions XAUT as tokenized ownership of physical gold. Each token is marketed as one troy ounce of gold, with specific bar ownership tracked via on-chain records.

Kinesis Gold (KAU)

Kinesis presents KAU as fully allocated gold with redemption pathways. The project emphasizes allocation, storage custody, and the ability to convert holdings to physical gold (subject to terms).

Comtech Gold (CGO)

Comtech markets CGO as a gold-backed token with a focus on accessibility and liquidity. Review reserve disclosures and redemption requirements to understand how backing is maintained.

Kinesis Silver (KAG)

Kinesis positions KAG as tokenized, allocated silver. The platform also advertises yield mechanics; confirm eligibility, redemption minimums, and fee structure before relying on any yield expectations.

Meld Gold (GOLD$)

Meld Gold describes GOLD$ as a tokenized gold product on Algorand, typically framed as 1 gram of gold per token. The project emphasizes settlement efficiency and low transaction costs.

VNX Gold (VNXAU)

VNX describes VNXAU as a Liechtenstein-issued token backed by LBMA-certified gold, commonly presented as 1 gram per token. Verify custody arrangements and audit cadence.

Perth Mint Gold Token (PMGT)

PMGT is marketed as a regulated gold token associated with the Perth Mint ecosystem and government-linked oversight. Confirm current issuance status, storage terms, and redemption processes.

AurusGOLD (AWG)

Aurus markets AWG as a 1-gram gold token backed by high-purity, LBMA-certified bullion. Review the token’s reserve reports and redemption conditions to validate backing claims.

Kinka (XNK)

XNK is described as a gold-linked token with a 1-gram framing. Confirm the issuer, custody model, and whether redemption is available in practice (not just in marketing).

Key Considerations

Before you trust a “metal-backed” token

Redeemability

Most reputable tokens allow for redemption, though minimum amounts may apply (e.g., 200 ounces for KAG).

Transparency

Look for projects that provide regular, independent third-party audits of their physical reserves.

Storage & Regulation

Gold is usually stored in secure, insured vaults (e.g., in Switzerland or London) and sometimes regulated by entities like the NYDFS.

Risks

Investors face risks related to vault security, the bankruptcy of the issuer, or fraudulent projects that do not hold adequate reserves.
